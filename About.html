Hello, my name is Louis! I was formerly a dental student at Boston University, and I have now transitioned into the role of a UX researcher. My passion lies in the realm of research.

During my undergraduate years, I delved into molecular research, focusing on the two genes, GADD34 and CReP, and ER stress. This experience honed my skills in data analysis and extracting valuable insights from academic journals. I firmly believe that thorough research forms the bedrock of exceptional design. A comprehensive understanding derived from robust research and data collection is pivotal in creating an outstanding end product. My time in dental school significantly boosted my critical thinking skills, especially as I delved into evidence-based dentistry research.

Understanding the logic and reasoning behind everything has always been my preferred way of doing things. My transition into UX aligns perfectly with my goals and also allows me to leverage my scientific methodologies and experiences from the past to enhance user experiences. I am dedicated to further hone my skills in research and am excited about the journey ahead, embracing the opportunities to learn in this ever-evovling field.
