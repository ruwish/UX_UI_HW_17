About Me

Hello, my name is Louis! I was formerly a dental student at Boston University, and I have now transitioned into the role of a UX researcher. I am capable of user research, prototyping, wireframing, and UI design.

My journey in research started off during my undergraduate years, which I delved into molecular biology research under the guidance of my biochemistry professor. It was during those time where I honed my skills in analysis, data interpretation, and critical thinking. I was able to learn the importance of rigor and thoroughness, which are skills that can transfer to the meticulous nature of UX. These foundational skills formed the bedrock of my transition into UX research, allowing me to shift my focus from microscopic level to understanding and improving user needs.

As a former dental student and someone who worked in the field of medicine, I witnessed firsthand the significance of user centric design in patient care. Combining my academic background, passion in research, and empathy for end users, I want to be able to make meaningful changes and bring positive impact to people's daily lives. Embracing a growth mindset, I look forward to the endless possibilities and opportunities to refine my skills.
